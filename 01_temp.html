Max Temperature


The temperature data shows the logical decrease in temperature with distance from the equator. In this dataset, the temperatures in the southern hemisphere appear to be warmer than the northern hemisphere; this makes sense for the time of year (mid-autumn north of the equator, and mid-spring south). Additionally The shape of the plot is interesting in that there appear to be a more measurements with a greater temperature variation north of the Equator. This can be explained by the fact that most of Earth's land mass is north of the Equator, and the land mass is more evenly distributed from Equator to Pole in the northern hemisphere. This is in contrast to the southern hemisphere, where much more of the earth's surface is covered with water, especially near the South Pole.
